Platform Command Manual

A comprehensive, end-to-step guide to the GenQuantis Discovery ecosystem. Designed for both professional researchers and platform administrators.

01. Dashboard Central

The main control hub where global platform metrics and experiment feeds are aggregated.

Non-Scientist View:

Think of this as the "Mission Control" center. It shows you how many medicine projects are running and if the system is healthy.

Scientist View:

Aggregated GNN inference metrics, success probability distribution, and real-time computation logs from the backend engine.

Interactive
The main dashboard interface showing throughput metrics and success rates.

How to use the Dashboard:

1
Check the Primary Cards for total analyzed molecules.
2
Scroll to Ongoing Discoveries to see the live feed of tasks.
3
Click on any Target Name in the table to jump to its 3D structural page.

02. Account & Settings

Manage your research profile, update your name, and configure your session details.

The profile management interface for updating researcher identity.

Updating your Profile:

1
Click Settings in the sidebar.
2
In the "Full Name" box, type your name (e.g., Dr. Ashwin Kumar).
3
Click Save Changes. Your name will update on the dashboard greeting immediately.

03. Target Explorer

Structural retrieval engine to fetch and visualize 3D protein structures.

Step 1: Locate the search field to enter your Protein or Gene identifier.

Search Instructions:

1
Type a Gene Symbol like EGFR or PDB ID like 5CWZ.
2
Click LOAD. The system will download the structure from the PDB database.
Step 2: The 3D viewer renders the protein. Use your mouse to rotate and zoom.

04. Pocket Identification

Mapping the geometric cavities of a protein to find where a drug can bind.

Real-time feedback: The AI scans the surface curvature of the protein.
Outcome: A table of binding sites ranked by Druggability Probability.

05. AI Hit Screening

Massive virtual screening to find the best candidate molecules from millions of options.

Purpose:

Evaluate millions of potential drug molecules against your target protein in minutes using GNN (Graph Neural Network) inference.

AI Logic:

The system computes spatial compatibility and electrostatic interactions to assign a 'Match Score'. Higher scores indicate a stronger binding potential.

Active Session: High-throughput virtual screening results and match scores.

Screening Workflow:

1
Select Library: Choose from Enamine, Zinc20, or your custom internal library.
2
Define Thresholds: Set the minimum binding score (e.g., >0.85) to filter candidates.
3
Execute Scan: Click "Start AI Screening" to trigger the cloud compute cluster.
4
Export Hits: Select the top-performing molecules and send them to the Molecular Docking module for physics-based validation.

06. Molecular Docking

Physics-based simulation of how a molecule fits into a protein pocket.

Simplification:

Think of this as testing a "Key" (Medicine) in a "Lock" (Protein) to see if it turns perfectly.

Input: Enter the SMILES code (chemical string) and click RUN DOCKING.
Result: The Binding Energy (Potency) is displayed. Lower numbers are better.

07. Lead Optimization

Generative AI models that "evolve" a molecule to make it more potent.

Evolution Setup: The AI will generate new versions of your starting molecule.
Progress Tracking: Watch the Potency Chart go up as the AI improves the lead.

08. ADMET Prediction

Checking if the medicine is safe for the human body and if it can be absorbed.

Safety Profile: The Radar chart shows absorption, safety, and toxicity levels.

09. Preformulation

Analyzing the physical properties like solubility and stability.

Stability Assessment:

Before a drug is made into a pill, we must know if it will dissolve in water or break down in the heat. This session simulates those physical properties.

Thermodynamic Profiling:

Calculation of LogP, pKa, and solubility constants (LogS) across a pH range of 1.0 to 9.0.

Working Session: Structural stability analysis and physical property profiling.
Extended Analysis: Solvent compatibility and degradation studies.

Analyzing Results:

1
Review the Solubility Curve to identify optimal delivery methods.
2
Check the Hygroscopicity Index to determine storage requirements.
3
Use the "Compare Leads" button to see which candidate has the best physical profile for manufacturing.

10. Formulation Design

Designing the final pill or injection using AI-optimized ingredient ratios.

Recipe Building:

Creating the perfect 'recipe' for the medicine. This ensures the drug is delivered to the right part of the body at the right time.

Excipient Compatibility:

AI-driven selection of binders, disintegrants, and lubricants based on the chemical scaffold of the lead molecule.

Optimization Session: AI-calculated ingredient ratios and stability predictions.

Design Steps:

1
Enter the Target Dosage (e.g., 50mg) and preferred form (Tablet/Capsule).
2
Run In-Silico Dissolution testing to predict drug release patterns.
3
Adjust Excipient Ratios manually or let the AI optimize for maximum shelf-life.

11. Blinded Results

A secure area where molecules are anonymized to prevent research bias.

Unbiased Review:

To ensure scientists aren't biased, we hide the names of the medicines. We only look at how well they work.

Statistical Significance:

Comparison of p-values and confidence intervals across blinded candidate groups A, B, and C.

Working Comparison: Anonymized performance metrics for unbiased lead selection.

How to interpret results:

1
Locate the Correlation Matrix to see which blinded groups perform similarly.
2
Assign a Priority Score to each anonymized candidate.
3
Click "Reveal Identity" only after the final selection committee has voted on the data.

12. Robotic Validation

Moving from the computer to the real world: Generating scripts for laboratory robots.

Hardware Setup: Configuring the experiment name and type for the robotic arm.
Outcome: The AI generates the Python code that tells the robot exactly what to do.

13. Discovery Reports

How to download and share your final intelligence reports.

Exporting Data: Click the "Export Report" button on the Target Explorer or Dashboard.

What's inside the report?

The report includes all 3D snapshots, binding energies, safety scores, and the generated robotic protocol.

14. Workflow Shortcuts

Special buttons that let you jump between different parts of the platform instantly.

Jump to Lab: From Lead Optimization, click "Validate in Wet Lab" to skip the manual setup.
Jump to Docking: From the Lab, click "Send to Docking" to re-verify the binding fit.
